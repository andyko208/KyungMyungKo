I'm an undergraduate student at Purdue University, majoring in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence. I come from diverse research background. I recently did my research internship at INAOE, Mexico through NSF IRES, working with Professor Manuel Montes-y-Gómez on multimodal & multilingual hate speech detection. Before that, I worked with Professor James Davis to analyze the qualities of signatures on open source registries. Even before that, I worked on developing multimodal EEG interface at tiilt lab as a research intern, working with Professor Marcelo Worsley.

Research Interests

  • Multimodal learning: Vision and audio are the primary sources of data that we continuosly perceive from the world. Our brain leverages these cues to reason about them. As such, our world could be explained as us humans experiencing immersive video. My research attempts to understand how these modalities are incorporated to create individual world within us. Ultimately, my goal is to leverage them to construct a synthetic world. I am specifically interested in video understanding and video generation.
